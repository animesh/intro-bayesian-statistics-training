Bayes theorem

Conditional probability

P(B∣A)=P(A∩B)P(A)P(B∣A)=P(A∩B)P(A)

  • we rescale or renormalise to only AA events

Rearrange

P(B∣A)P(A)=P(A∩B)=P(A∣B)P(B)P(B∣A)P(A)=P(A∩B)=P(A∣B)P(B)

Bayes theorem

P(B∣A)=P(A∣B)P(B)P(A),P(A)=P(A∣B)P(B)+P(A∣¬B)P(¬B)P(B∣A)=P(A∣B)P(B)P(A),P(A)=P(A∣B)P(B)+P(A∣¬B)P(¬B)

What’s the chance the winning family has two daughters? Answer: ≈13≈13.

Or we can simulate, or compute:

n_fam <- 1e5 # number of families
first_child <- sample(c("D", "S"), 
                n_fam, replace = TRUE)
second_child <- sample(c("D", "S"), 
                n_fam, replace = TRUE)
families <- cbind(first_child, second_child)
# remove those with two sons
two_sons <- which(rowSums(families == "S") == 2)
selected_families <- families[-two_sons, ]
# work out the fraction with two daughters
p_two_daughters <- mean(rowSums(
  selected_families == "D") == 2)
# print the simulation results
signif(p_two_daughters, 3)

Bayes theorem and pp-values

The pp-value is the probability of the data (or more extreme) given the null hypothesis

p=P(D+∣H0)p=P(D+∣H0)

We would like to know the probability of the hypothesis given the data: P(H0∣D)P(H0∣D)

P(H0∣D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)P(D)P(H0∣D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)P(D)

  • Bayes theorem

Need to know:

  • the prior probability of the null hypothesis: P(H0)P(H0)
  • P(D)P(D)

P(D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)+P(D∣¬H0)P(¬H0)P(D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)+P(D∣¬H0)P(¬H0)

  • the likelihood of the data under all other hypotheses: P(D∣¬H0)P(D∣¬H0)

The pp-value does not include this information.

Summary

pp-values are a measure of surprise

  • probability of the data statistic (sample mean) or more extreme given the null

Commonly misinterpreted

  • not the probability of the hypothesis given the data!

Confidence intervals are the central part of estimates of the distribution of the statistic (sample mean)

  • contain similar information to the pp-value but even more commonly misinterpreted

To “invert” conditional probabilities

  • Bayes theorem

P(H0∣D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)P(D),P(D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)+P(D∣¬H0)P(¬H0)P(H0∣D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)P(D),P(D)=P(D∣H0)P(H0)+P(D∣¬H0)P(¬H0)

Need information beyond the pp-value: the prior H0H0 and to consider all alternative hypotheses!
