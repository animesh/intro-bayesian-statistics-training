Monte Carlo approximation

In general, these integrals are expectations

E[g(x)]=∫g(x)p(x)dxE[g(x)]=∫g(x)p(x)dx

If we can sample proportionally to p(x)p(x)

  • can use Monte Carlo approximation

E[g(x)]≈1M∑i=1Mg(xi)E[g(x)]≈1M∑i=1Mg(xi)

  • for MM random samples xixi, sampled ∼p(x)∼p(x)

Accuracy typically scales like 1M√1M for any dimension

  • number of bins for numerical integration grows exponentially in the dimension

Lies behind lots of Bayesian analyses

Question: how do we get samples from arbitrary distributions?

MCMC

Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) Metropolis, Rosenbluth××2, Teller××2 (1953); Hastings (1970)

To sample proportionally to any function p(x)p(x):

[1.] From current position xx, propose a new position x′x′

x′=x+δ,δ∼q(d)x′=x+δ,δ∼q(d)

  • with a symmetric distribution qq(d), eg a normal (can modify algorithm for asymmetric too)

[2.] Accept x′x′ with probability

min(1,p(x′)p(x))min(1,p(x′)p(x))

  • and move current position to x′x′
  • otherwise stay at xx

[3.] Repeat, that’s all!

  • (though it may take many iterations to converge)

HMC

Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC) Duane, Kennedy, Pendleton and Roweth (1987); Neal (1995, 2011)

To sample proportionally to any function p(x)p(x): add a new dimension ρρ!

Make ρρ Gaussian, and define the negative log-likelihood as an energy surface

H(x,ρ)=−log{p(x)}+ρ22H(x,ρ)=−log⁡{p(x)}+ρ22

[1.] From current position xx, sample a Gaussian ρρ and propose a new pair (x′,ρ′)(x′,ρ′) by propagating (numerically) under the (energy-preserving) Hamiltonian dynamics: dxdt=∂H∂ρ,dρdt=−∂H∂xdxdt=∂H∂ρ,dρdt=−∂H∂x

[2.] Accept x′x′ with probability

min(1,exp{H(x,ρ)−H(x′,ρ′)})≈1min(1,exp⁡{H(x,ρ)−H(x′,ρ′)})≈1

  • and move current position to x′x′
  • otherwise stay at xx

[3.] Repeat, that’s all!

Proposed x: 0.545203, proposed ρ: -1.300332
Acceptance probability: 1
